Tigers

The tiger (Panthera tigris) is the largest cat species and one of the most iconic predators in the world. It is known for its striking orange coat with black stripes, powerful build, and solitary nature. - Size: Tigers can grow up to 3.3 meters (11 feet) in length and weigh up to 300 kg (660 lbs). - Habitat: Found across Asia, from India and China to Siberia and Sumatra. - Diet: Tigers are carnivores, primarily hunting deer, wild boar, and buffalo. - Behavior: They are solitary hunters, using stealth and strength to ambush prey. - Subspecies: Includes the Bengal, Siberian, Sumatran, Malayan, Indochinese, and South China tigers. - Conservation: Tigers are endangered, with fewer than 4,000 left in the wild due to habitat loss and poaching. üêÖ - Behavior: They are solitary hunters, using stealth and strength to ambush prey. - Subspecies: Includes the Bengal, Siberian, Sumatran, Malayan, Indochinese, and South China tigers.
