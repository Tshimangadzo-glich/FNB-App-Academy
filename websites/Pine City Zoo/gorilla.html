Gorilla

The gorilla (Gorilla) is the largest primate and one of our closest relatives, sharing 96–99% of human DNA. These powerful yet gentle creatures are native to the tropical forests of equatorial Africa. - Species: There are two main species—the western gorilla (Gorilla gorilla) and the eastern gorilla (Gorilla beringei)—each with subspecies. - Size: Males, known as silverbacks, can weigh up to 270 kg (600 lbs) and stand 1.8 meters (5 ft 11 in) tall.

- Diet: Gorillas are primarily herbivorous, feeding on leaves, fruits, and stems - Social Structure: They live in troops led by a dominant silverback, who protects and leads the group.

- Intelligence: Gorillas use tools, communicate through gestures and vocalizations, and display complex emotions. - Conservation: Sadly, gorillas are critically endangered due to habitat destruction, poaching, and disease.
