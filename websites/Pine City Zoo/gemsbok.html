GEMSBOK

- Appearance: Gemsbok have a gray-brown coat with bold black-and-white facial markings and long, straight horns. - Both males and females have horns, but females' tend to be thinner and longer. - - Size: They stand about 1.2 meters (4 feet) at the shoulder, with males weighing between 180–240 kg (400–530 lbs) and females 100–210 kg (220–460 lbs) - Diet: Primarily grass-eaters, but they also dig for roots and tubers to extract moisture, allowing them to survive in extreme desert conditions. - -

- Behavior: Gemsbok live in herds of 10 to 40 animals and can reach speeds of 60 km/h (37 mph) -

- Lifespan: They can live up to 20 years in the wild.

WHERE TO FIND THEM?

You will find the Gemsbok at pen E12, next to the first enterence.
